Confidential - M&A Due Diligence

RoomBoss Acquisition

Strategic questions for CEO meeting based on comprehensive API analysis, competitive benchmarking, and product gap assessment.

📅 December 2024
📋 30 Questions
🎯 6 Categories

🔍 Pre-Meeting Context

Our technical analysis identified the following key findings that inform these questions:

  • 23 API endpoints available across 3 APIs (GS Purchasing, Hotel, OTA Rates)
  • 18 critical gaps vs. competitors like Duve (mobile check-in, upselling, messaging, analytics)
  • No webhook/event system - prevents proactive automation
  • No reporting API - analytics requires custom data warehouse
  • XML-based OTA API - older standard vs. modern REST/GraphQL
🔌

API & Technical Capabilities

Questions 1-6
1
What is your API roadmap for the next 12-24 months? Specifically, are there plans to add webhooks/event notifications for booking changes, check-ins, and payments? Critical
Why we ask: Our AI agents need real-time event triggers for proactive guest communication. Currently we'd have to poll your APIs constantly, which is inefficient and introduces latency.
2
We noticed no dedicated reporting/analytics API. Is there an enterprise data export capability, or plans to expose aggregated metrics (RevPAR, ADR, occupancy) via API? Critical
Why we ask: Competitors like Mews provide real-time analytics APIs. We need this for our Revenue Intelligence agent (REN) to deliver value.
3
The OTA Rates API uses XML/OpenTravel format. Are there plans to modernize to REST/JSON, or is there a parallel modern API available to enterprise customers? High
Why we ask: XML parsing adds complexity and slows integration development. JSON APIs are 3-5x faster to implement.
4
What's the current API rate limiting policy? Can we negotiate higher limits or dedicated infrastructure for enterprise deployments?
Why we ask: AI agents may generate high API volume during peak check-in times. We need to ensure we won't be throttled.
5
Is there a sandbox/staging environment with realistic test data for API development? What's the process for testing new integrations?
Why we ask: We need a safe environment to test AI agent workflows without affecting production bookings.
6
What's the typical API uptime SLA, and what redundancy/failover systems are in place? Have there been any significant outages in the past 24 months?
Why we ask: Our AI concierge will be guest-facing 24/7. API downtime directly impacts guest experience.
🚀

Product & Feature Gaps

Questions 7-12
7
Duve and Canary offer mobile check-in with digital key integration. Is this on RoomBoss's roadmap, or is this intentionally left to partners? Critical
Why we ask: 39% of luxury guests expect mobile check-in. This is a table-stakes feature for our target market.
8
We see no upselling/offer management functionality in the API. Is there an internal system for this, or how do your customers currently handle dynamic upselling? Critical
Why we ask: Upselling drives $17K+/month per property for competitors. This is our biggest revenue opportunity gap.
9
What guest messaging capabilities exist within RoomBoss? Is there any WhatsApp/SMS integration, or is communication handled externally? Critical
Why we ask: Omnichannel messaging is core to modern guest experience. Duve's AI resolves tickets in under 1 minute via integrated messaging.
10
The housekeeping API only allows status updates. Is there a task management system internally? Any plans to expose task creation, assignment, and tracking via API? High
Why we ask: Our Operations agent (KENJI) needs to automate housekeeping workflows and dispatch tasks to staff mobile apps.
11
How do RoomBoss customers currently handle spa and F&B reservations? Is there internal functionality not exposed via API, or do they use third-party systems?
Why we ask: We need unified booking for all guest services - accommodation, spa, dining, activities - from one AI interface.
12
Is there a guest profile/preference system that persists across stays? Can we store and retrieve guest preferences (room temp, pillow type, dietary restrictions)?
Why we ask: Personalization requires remembering guest preferences. This is fundamental to luxury hospitality AI.
🔗

Integration Ecosystem

Questions 13-17
13
How many active integrations does RoomBoss currently have? Is there a partner/marketplace program we could leverage or expand?
Why we ask: Duve has 150+ integrations, Mews has 600+. Integration breadth determines platform stickiness.
14
Which digital key providers (ASSA ABLOY, Salto, etc.) have existing integrations with RoomBoss? What would be required to add mobile key support? High
Why we ask: Mobile keys are essential for contactless check-in. We need to understand the integration path.
15
What payment gateway integrations exist? Can we process upsell payments, deposits, and refunds programmatically with full PCI compliance?
Why we ask: Seamless payment processing is critical for upselling and automated deposit collection.
16
Which channel managers are integrated? How does RoomBoss handle rate parity and inventory sync across OTAs?
Why we ask: Our Revenue Intelligence agent needs visibility into channel performance and rate positioning.
17
Is there any existing AI/chatbot integration, or has RoomBoss explored partnerships with conversational AI platforms?
Why we ask: We want to understand if there's existing AI infrastructure we can build upon or if we're starting fresh.

Business & Market Position

Questions 18-23
18
What is RoomBoss's current market share in Japan, particularly in the ski resort / luxury boutique segment?
Why we ask: We're focused on Niseko and Japanese luxury hospitality. Local market strength matters.
19
Who do you consider your primary competitors in Asia-Pacific? How do you differentiate against Mews, Cloudbeds, or local players?
Why we ask: Understanding competitive positioning helps us assess market defensibility post-acquisition.
20
What's the current customer count, and what's the breakdown by property type (hotels, vacation rentals, hostels) and size?
Why we ask: Customer concentration and segment mix affect valuation and growth strategy.
21
What's the average revenue per customer, and what's the pricing model (per room, flat fee, transaction-based)?
Why we ask: We need to understand unit economics and how pricing would work for our AI platform overlay.
22
What's the customer churn rate, and what are the primary reasons customers leave?
Why we ask: Churn reasons often reveal product gaps. If customers leave for Duve/Canary features, that validates our thesis.
23
Are there any existing white-label or reseller agreements? What flexibility exists for us to brand the platform for our managed properties?
Why we ask: We want to offer a branded "The 1898 Niseko" experience, not expose RoomBoss branding to guests.

Technical Infrastructure

Questions 24-27
24
What's the technology stack? Is the platform cloud-native, and where is data hosted (important for Japan data residency)?
Why we ask: Japan has data localization preferences. Cloud architecture affects scalability and our ability to extend the platform.
25
What's the engineering team size and composition? Are there dedicated API/platform engineers, or is it primarily product-focused?
Why we ask: We need to assess capacity to build the API enhancements we require post-acquisition.
26
Is there technical debt that's blocking feature development? What would you prioritize if you had 2x engineering resources?
Why we ask: Honest assessment of technical debt helps us plan post-acquisition investments.
27
What security certifications does RoomBoss hold (SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI DSS)? How is guest PII protected?
Why we ask: Security compliance is non-negotiable for luxury hospitality. We need to ensure enterprise-grade standards.

Strategic Vision

Questions 28-30
28
Duve just raised $60M to build AI-native guest experience. What's RoomBoss's AI strategy? Is this a priority, or is the focus elsewhere? Critical
Why we ask: This reveals whether our vision aligns. If AI isn't a priority for RoomBoss, we'd be building it ourselves post-acquisition.
29
If we acquire RoomBoss with the goal of building the leading AI-powered hospitality platform for Asia-Pacific luxury properties, what would you see as the biggest obstacles?
Why we ask: CEO perspective on challenges is invaluable. They know where the bodies are buried.
30
What would success look like for you personally in this acquisition? Would you be interested in staying on to help realize the AI-native vision?
Why we ask: Founder/CEO retention can be critical for continuity. Understanding their motivation helps structure the deal.

🚨 Potential Deal Breakers to Probe

❌ No webhook roadmap - If events aren't planned, we can't build proactive AI
❌ Closed architecture - If we can't extend APIs, we're locked in
❌ No data export - If we can't access historical data, BI is impossible
❌ Technical debt crisis - If core platform needs rewrite, timeline explodes
❌ Key customer concentration - If top 3 customers = 50%+ revenue, risk is high
❌ Misaligned vision - If CEO doesn't believe in AI-native future, cultural clash awaits

📝 Negotiation Leverage Points

  • API gaps are opportunities: Position our AI layer as the solution to their competitive disadvantage vs. Duve/Canary
  • We bring distribution: Our hotel management contracts guarantee deployment to properties
  • Engineering investment: We can accelerate their roadmap with dedicated AI/API engineering
  • Market expansion: Our Niseko presence opens luxury ski resort vertical across Japan
  • If they're defensive about gaps: Emphasize partnership to solve together, not criticism
